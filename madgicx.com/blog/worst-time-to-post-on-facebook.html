What's the worst time to post on Facebook?

According to the latest data for 2025, here are some of the worst times to post on Facebook:

1. Late nights and early mornings (8 pm - 8 am): Most people are asleep during these hours, resulting in minimal engagement. Even night owls will likely be engaged in other activities rather than scrolling through their Facebook feed.

2. Weekends (particularly Sundays): Weekend engagement tends to be lower as people are often busy with personal activities, family time, or outdoor pursuits. Sunday mornings, in particular, see meager engagement rates as many people are either sleeping in or attending religious services.

It's important to note that these times can vary depending on your specific audience and industry. For instance, a nightclub might find late-night posts more effective than a B2B software company. The key is to understand your unique audience and their online behaviors.

How Facebook's algorithm dictates visibility

Understanding Facebook's algorithm is crucial for maximizing your post's reach and engagement. The algorithm prioritizes content based on several factors:

1. Relevance: How closely the content matches the user's interests. Facebook analyzes user behavior, including past interactions, to determine the content most relevant to each individual.

2. Engagement: The number of likes, comments, and shares a post receives determines its reach. Posts with higher engagement get shown to a broader audience.

3. Timeliness: How recent the post is. Facebook tends to prioritize newer content, although highly engaging older posts can still appear in users' feeds.

4. Relationship: The strength of the connection between the user and the content creator. Users are likelier to see content from friends, family, and Pages they interact with frequently.

5. Post type: Different types of content (video, images, text) get favored at different times. Facebook often prefers native videos and live streams.

6. Time spent: The time users spend viewing a post. Posts that keep users engaged for extended periods are likelier to be shown to others.

7. Completion: For video content, Facebook‚Äôs algorithm favors videos that viewers watch to the end. It‚Äôs best to keep your videos short and engaging to ensure people watch as much as possible.

By considering these factors and avoiding the worst posting times, you can increase the chances of your target audience seeing and engaging your content. For inspiration, check out these Facebook ad examples to see how successful brands craft content to align with these algorithmic preferences.

Understand when your audience is online

While general advice on the best and worst times to post on Facebook can be helpful, it's important to remember that what's golden for one might be a dud for another. Time zones, industries, and your unique audience all play a role. Business owners and social media managers must dig into their data to determine when their crowd is most active.

Here are some steps to help you determine the best (and worst) times for your Facebook posts:

1. Use Facebook Insights: This free tool provides valuable data about when your followers are online and most active. It shows user activity breakdown by day and hour, allowing you to pinpoint optimal posting times.

2. Experiment with posting times: Try posting at different times and days of the week, then analyze the engagement rates for each post. This A/B testing approach can reveal surprising insights about your audience's behavior.

3. Consider your audience demographics: Different age groups and professions may have varying social media habits. For example, younger audiences might be more active in the evenings, while professionals might engage more during lunch breaks.

4. Monitor your competitors: Look at when your competitors are posting and how their content performs. While you shouldn't copy their strategy outright, it can provide valuable insights into industry trends.

5. Use social media management tools: These can provide more in-depth analytics about your post performance and audience behavior. Many tools offer features like heat maps that visualize your best posting times.

6. Account for time zones: Consider this when scheduling your posts if your audience spans multiple time zones. You should post the same content at different times to reach all audience segments effectively.

7. Analyze seasonal trends: Your optimal posting times might change with the seasons and trends. For example, people might see more evening activity in the summer as they enjoy longer daylight hours.

It's worth noting that while this approach is crucial for organic posts, it's equally important for paid advertising on Facebook. Understanding when your audience is most active and engaged can help optimize ad spend and improve your return on investment (ROI).

What is the best time to post on Facebook?

While understanding the worst times to post is crucial, knowing the best times to maximize your engagement is equally important. According to the latest data for 2025, here are some of the best times to post on Facebook:

1. Mondays from 9 am to 12 pm: Most people are active on Facebook during work breaks or lunch hours. There's often a peak in activity around 11 AM as people take a mid-morning break.

2. Tuesdays and Thursdays from 9 am to 2 pm and 5 pm: Consistent engagement throughout the day until lunchtime, with another spike in the afternoon.

3. Wednesdays from 9 am to 3 pm and 5 pm: Mid-week tends to see high engagement rates. Wednesday is the best day to post on Facebook, with a notable spike in activity well past lunchtime and 5 pm onwards.

4. Fridays from 9 am to 11 am: Many people are wrapping up their week on Fridays after lunchtime, which can lead to a focus on completing tasks rather than browsing social media.

5. Saturdays from 8 am to 11 am: As we‚Äôve already mentioned, weekends are generally slower on Facebook, but if you need to post on a Saturday, aim for early morning. People often start their day leisurely, checking their phones while still in bed, making this a prime window to catch their attention before they get busy with weekend plans.

6. Sundays from 9 am to 11 am: Sundays are the worst days for posting. However, the early morning is your best bet, depending on your industry. Many users browse Facebook before heading out for the day‚Äôs activities, giving you a small window of opportunity to reach them before they're off to brunch, church, or other Sunday commitments.

What is the best time to post on Instagram?

While focusing on Facebook, it's worth noting that Instagram (owned by Meta) has its own optimal posting times. Here's what the data suggests for Instagram in 2025:

1. Monday and Thursday from 11 am to 12 pm: Lunchtime scrolling is standard on Instagram. Many Instagram users check their feeds during their lunch break, making this a prime time for engagement.

2. Tuesdays from 9 am to 10 am: Mid-morning engagement tends to be high as people start their day. Many users check Instagram early in the morning while easing into their workday or during a quick coffee break.

3. Wednesdays at 11 am: Mid-week late mornings see a spike in Instagram activity, likely due to users looking for a quick mid-week break or inspiration to get through the rest of the week.

4. Fridays around 4 pm: People are excited for the weekend and often check Instagram for weekend plans, inspiration, or just to wind down after the workweek.

5. Saturday at 9 am: Many users are awake, relaxed, and lazily scrolling through their feeds before heading out for the day.

6. Sunday evening around 7 pm: Sunday evenings are prime time for people to unwind, prepare for the week ahead, and casually scroll through social media platforms from the comfort of home.
